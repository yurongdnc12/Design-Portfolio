Making design changes before launch of LiveMore


Role: Designer (Solo)

Background

Joining a new company a couple of weeks before the product launch was an interesting personal experience to me, as most startups I have worked with already have launched their products in the market. As such, I decided to pen down some of the improvements I proposed to the product, hoping that it would better prepare the product for its launch.

There were prior feedback given by testers (not using user research methods but more of "solutioning-suggestions"), and I did not refer to these to propose any new product features before the launch. But when there were problems raised by testers, I tried to understand the story behind the problem they faced, and why the experience was poor on their end. I made slight improvements to these pages, as explained below.

About LiveMore

LiveMore is a free self-development app providing content from experts worldwide to help people implement positive changes to their life. We provide 3 types of content - namely Short Reads, Quizzes and Tracks.

Home Page

After joining LiveMore, one of the most prominent problems pointed out was the Home Page.

As mentioned by several testers, the problems with existing home page design are:

• New users "don’t know where to start" -> lack of clear entry point

• Hard to find all the tracks (where they have to click on the Develop tab then "See All")

• Lack of the LiveMore brand identity

In order to solve the problem of missing clear entry point and poor accessibility of tracks, I designed the new home page to clearly state the 3 different types of content – Short Reads, Quizzes and Tracks.

To also improve the sense of brand identity, I have added the LiveMore logo, colours and fonts accordingly. Since we were using a different UI framework for iOS and Android, I also tweaked certain design elements that were individually unique to iOS or Android, to elements that look common to both OS.

Onboarding screens

Onboarding assists users in understanding what is LiveMore about, before they decide whether to sign up.

At this point, it felt like a good opportunity for us to consider our colour palette, and decide the colours for different UI elements such as the primary and secondary buttons, hyperlinks etc. Prior to thi we had been using the default OS colours and elements. Working with orange and turqoise blue was challenging as it was tough to find darker shades of these colours which actually look good on mobile interfaces.

Quiz results page

As mentioned by several users, some problems with the previous quiz result page were

• What does the absolute score mean? How does it help me to understand myself better?

• They didn’t see the recommended tracks below

As such, the new page design aims to improve the experience by:

• Showing the maximum score of the quiz (The team previously discussed to recalibrate all maximum scores to 100, but since not all the maximum scores of Quizzes are 100, this means that we have to recalculate all the ranges of scores experts have provided us – which is very time consuming)

• Have a short description of ‘who you are’ – “You are moderately active” etc. to clearly and concisely explain to the users what their results mean

• Show the score range which users falls in

• Add "Recommended tracks"/ "What might interest you"
